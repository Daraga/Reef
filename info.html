Save the Reef Information

Facts

  • The Great Barrier Reef, located off the coast of Queensland, Australia, is the largest coral reef system in the world.
  • It is home to a wide range of life, including fish, sea turtles, giant clams, seahorses, sea snakes, nudibranch, stingrays, sharks and many more.
  • Climate change poses the greatest threat to the Great Barrier Reef - an increase in ocean temperatures and acidity levels create heat stress and decrease the capacity for corals to build their indispensable skeletons.
