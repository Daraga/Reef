Facts

The Great Barrier Reef, located off the coast of Queensland, Australia, is the largest coral reef system in the world. It is home to a wide range of life, including fish, sea turtles, giant clams, seahorses, sea snakes, nudibranch, sea turtles, stingrays, sharks and many more. Climate change poses the greatest threat to the Great Barrier Reef - an increase in ocean temperatures and acidity levels create heat stress and decrease the capacity for corals to build their indispensable skeletons.

  • Here are some ways you can help save the reef:
  • Don’t litter
  • If you dive, don't touch
  • Conserve water (i.e: use reusable bottles and hang your hotel towels so they don’t get washed every day)
  • Use environmentally-friendly modes of transportation
  • Volunteer with some of these organizations

About

We are #ReefsAreHome, a small organization trying to save the Great Barrier Reef. Learn about how this natural wonder affects us all and what you can do today to protect it. Our campaign goal is to bring awareness about how the reef is important for humans as well as animals and to try to bring our community together to save the Great Barrier. To learn more about the Great Barrier and what we do, follow our instagram account Instagram@ReefsAreHome. We post about what is currently happening and ideas on how you can contribute to our goal of Saving the Great Barrier Reef
